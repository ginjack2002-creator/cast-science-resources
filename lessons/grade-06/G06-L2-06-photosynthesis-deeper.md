## Platform

### Title
**The Invisible Plant Factory: Photosynthesis Deeper — How Plants Build Themselves from Thin Air and Sunlight**

### Overview
Students model photosynthesis as a three-input system where light, CO2, and water must ALL be present for glucose production. By discovering the limiting factor concept and the surprising truth that trees are made of AIR (carbon from CO2), students connect photosynthesis to the global carbon cycle and atmospheric oxygen maintenance.

### Cover Image
[A photorealistic macro photograph of a vibrant green leaf with visible cellular structure backlit by golden sunlight, water droplets on the surface catching light like jewels, dramatic botanical photography]

### Grade
6th (Level 2)

### NGSS Standard
**MS-LS1-6**: Construct a scientific explanation based on evidence for the role of photosynthesis in the cycling of matter and flow of energy into and out of organisms.
**MS-LS1-7**: Develop a model to describe how food is rearranged through chemical reactions forming new molecules that support growth and/or release energy as this matter moves through an organism.

### Learning Objectives
- Model how light intensity, CO2 concentration, and water availability all contribute to photosynthesis
- Explain how glucose production drives both oxygen release and plant biomass growth
- Demonstrate that the mass of a plant comes primarily from CO2 in the air, not from soil
- Predict how removing any one input stops the entire photosynthesis process
- Connect photosynthesis to the global carbon cycle and atmospheric oxygen

### Component List (Starting Model: 6 Components)

| Component | Type | What It Represents |
|-----------|------|-------------------|
| Light Intensity | External | The amount of sunlight energy reaching the leaf |
| CO2 Concentration | External | The amount of carbon dioxide available in the air |
| Water Availability | External | The amount of water reaching the leaf through roots |
| Glucose Production | Internal | The rate of sugar production from CO2, water, and light |
| Oxygen Released | Internal | The O2 produced as a byproduct of splitting water |
| Plant Biomass Growth | Internal | The increase in plant mass as glucose builds structures |

## Step 1: LOCATE — Build Your Photosynthesis System

### Text Editor

```
THE INVISIBLE PLANT FACTORY

A giant redwood tree weighs over 2 million pounds. It started as
a tiny seed smaller than your fingernail. Where did all that mass
come from? Most people say soil. They are wrong. A tree is made of
AIR. And you are about to prove it.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Task A: CHOOSE YOUR COMPONENTS
• EXTERNAL (supplied by the environment — THREE inputs):
  ○ "Light Intensity" — energy from the Sun
  ○ "CO2 Concentration" — carbon dioxide from the air
  ○ "Water Availability" — water from the soil via roots
• INTERNAL (processes and outputs within the plant):
  ○ "Glucose Production" — the sugar the plant makes
  ○ "Oxygen Released" — the gas we breathe (a byproduct!)
  ○ "Plant Biomass Growth" — the plant getting bigger

Task B: ADD TO YOUR MODEL (6 components — our most complex yet)

Task C: SORT — Three external inputs, three internal responses

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

THREE inputs must ALL be present. Remove any ONE and the entire
factory shuts down. That is the power of the limiting factor.
```

### Video Script

```
"This leaf is a solar-powered factory that builds itself from thin
air. Literally. And it has been doing this for 3 billion years.

A redwood tree weighs 2 million pounds. It started as a seed
smaller than your fingernail. Where did all that mass come from?

If I poll the class, most of you will say soil. That is wrong.
A tree is made of AIR. Carbon dioxide from the atmosphere. The
carbon atoms in CO2 become the carbon backbone of glucose, which
becomes cellulose, which becomes WOOD. A tree is literally built
from air and water, powered by sunlight. The soil provides only
minerals, less than 5 percent of the tree's mass.

Jan van Helmont proved this in 1648. He grew a willow tree for
5 years. The tree gained 164 pounds. The soil lost only 2 ounces.

Today you are going to model the system that makes this happen.
Three external inputs: Light Intensity, CO2 Concentration, Water
Availability. All three must be present. Remove any one and
photosynthesis stops cold.

Three internal outputs: Glucose Production, Oxygen Released,
Plant Biomass Growth.

This is our most complex model yet. Six components. Three inputs.
But the principle is simple: plants are solar-powered air factories.

Now it's your turn to ModelIt!"
```

## Step 2: ESTABLISH — Connect the Relationships

### Text Editor

```
THREE INPUTS, ONE PROCESS, THREE OUTPUTS

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Task A: DRAW YOUR RELATIONSHIPS
• Light Intensity → Glucose Production
• CO2 Concentration → Glucose Production
• Water Availability → Glucose Production
• Glucose Production → Oxygen Released
• Glucose Production → Plant Biomass Growth

Task B: SET ALL POSITIVE (+)
  ○ Light → Glucose = POSITIVE: More light = more photosynthesis
  ○ CO2 → Glucose = POSITIVE: More CO2 = more carbon to fix
  ○ Water → Glucose = POSITIVE: More water = more hydrogen atoms
  ○ Glucose → Oxygen = POSITIVE: More photosynthesis = more O2 released
  ○ Glucose → Biomass = POSITIVE: More glucose = more building material

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

ALL relationships are positive, but there is a hidden constraint:
the LIMITING FACTOR. The rate is controlled by whichever input
is in shortest supply, regardless of how abundant the others are.
```

### Video Script

```
"All three inputs converge on one central process: glucose production.
And all the relationships are positive.

More light powers more photosynthesis. More CO2 provides more carbon
atoms for fixation. More water provides more hydrogen atoms and
electrons. Each input increases glucose production.

Glucose then drives two outputs. Oxygen Released: oxygen is actually
a BYPRODUCT of splitting water molecules. Plants do not make oxygen
on purpose. It is leftover from cracking water apart. And Plant
Biomass Growth: glucose is used to build cellulose, lignin, proteins,
every structural molecule in the plant.

But here is the critical hidden rule: the LIMITING FACTOR. If you
max out light and water but CO2 is low, photosynthesis is limited
by the CO2. The other two inputs do not matter because the bottleneck
is CO2. This is like a recipe that needs three ingredients. Missing
ANY one means no cake, no matter how much of the other two you have.

This is why commercial greenhouses supplement CO2 to levels 2 to 3
times above normal atmospheric concentration. They figured out that
CO2 was the limiting factor and removed the bottleneck.

Now it's your turn to ModelIt!"
```

## Step 3: VISUALIZE & EVALUATE — Run Your Photosynthesis Model

### Text Editor

```
TIME TO RUN THE PLANT FACTORY

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Task A: SCENARIO 1 — FULL SUN (All inputs maximized)
• Set Light, CO2, and Water all to maximum
• Observe peak photosynthesis rates

Task B: SCENARIO 2 — DARKNESS (Lock Light to 0%)
• Keep CO2 and Water at normal levels
• What happens to EVERYTHING when light disappears?

Task C: SCENARIO 3 — DROUGHT (Lock Water to 10%)
• Keep Light and CO2 normal
• What happens even with plenty of light and CO2?

Task D: COMPARE
• Which shutdown is faster: darkness or drought?
• What is the limiting factor in each scenario?
• Does the plant produce ANY oxygen in darkness?

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

REFLECTION:
• Why is it surprising that most plant mass comes from air?
• If you increase light but not CO2, why does photosynthesis plateau?
• How is photosynthesis the reverse of cellular respiration?
```

### Video Script

```
"Full sun. All inputs maximized. Click Play. Watch the factory run
at peak capacity. Glucose Production is high. Oxygen Released is
high. Plant Biomass Growth is strong. Everything humming.

Now turn off the lights. Lock Light Intensity to zero. Keep CO2
and water normal. This is a dark closet.

Watch. Glucose Production drops to zero IMMEDIATELY. No light means
the light-dependent reactions cannot function. They cannot split
water. They cannot power anything. Everything stops.

Oxygen Released? Zero. No water splitting, no oxygen.

And Plant Biomass Growth? Not just zero. It goes NEGATIVE. Why?
Because the plant is still doing cellular respiration. It is
burning its stored glucose for survival. Without new glucose from
photosynthesis, it consumes its reserves. The plant is eating itself.

Now try drought. Lock Water to 10%. Light and CO2 are fine. But
Glucose Production drops significantly. Water is a direct reactant
in photosynthesis AND plants close their stomata to conserve water,
which also blocks CO2 entry. One shortage causes a cascade.

This is the limiting factor concept. It does not matter how much
light and CO2 you have. If water is scarce, that is the bottleneck.
The system runs at the speed of its slowest input.

And the biggest mind-blow: most of a tree's mass comes from CO2 in
the AIR. The carbon atoms in carbon dioxide become the carbon in
glucose become the carbon in cellulose become WOOD. A tree is
literally made of air. Van Helmont proved it in 1648.

Now it's your turn to ModelIt!"
```

```
2. What if you max out ONE input and minimize another?
3. Can you find the point where increasing light no longer helps?

WHAT'S MISSING?
• Temperature — enzymes work faster in warmth (to a point)
• Chlorophyll Concentration — pale leaves photosynthesize less
• Stomata Openness — open for CO2 in, but water vapor escapes

RESEARCH DIRECTIONS:
• Van Helmont's willow tree experiment
• Rising atmospheric CO2 and its effect on plant growth
• C3, C4, and CAM photosynthesis: desert adaptations
• How vertical farms optimize all three inputs

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

ADD ONE COMPONENT. Connect it. Run it. Which limiting factor
does it address?
```

### Video Script

```
"Your photosynthesis model captures the three-input system and the
limiting factor concept. Now think about optimizing.

Which input is most commonly the limiting factor in nature? In a
bright desert, water. In a dense forest understory, light. In a
sealed greenhouse, CO2. It depends on the environment.

Think about stomata. These are pores on leaves. Open, they let CO2
in for photosynthesis. But open stomata also let water vapor OUT.
In a hot, dry environment, plants face a brutal tradeoff: open
stomata for CO2 and lose water, or close stomata to save water
and starve for CO2.

Some plants evolved solutions. C4 plants like corn concentrate CO2
internally. CAM plants like cacti open stomata only at night when
it is cooler. Evolution solved the optimization problem.

Research one. Add it. Connect it. Test it. You are thinking like
a plant biologist now.

Now it's your turn to ModelIt!"
```

## CAST-Aligned Pre/Post Assessment

### Question 1
A student sorts photosynthesis model components. Which are external?
A. Light Intensity only  B. Light and CO2 only  C. Light Intensity, CO2 Concentration, and Water Availability  D. All six components
**Correct: C** — All three inputs come from the environment.

### Question 2
A scientist grows two plants. Plant A gets normal light, CO2, and water. Plant B gets normal light and water but NO CO2. After 4 weeks, what happens?
A. Plant B grows normally because it has light and water  B. Plant B grows very little or dies because CO2 provides the carbon atoms for glucose  C. Plant B grows faster because CO2 is toxic  D. Both plants grow the same
**Correct: B**

### Question 3
A student claims plants get food from soil. Using van Helmont's 1648 experiment (tree gained 164 lbs, soil lost 2 oz), which response is most accurate?
A. The student is correct because plants have roots in soil  B. The student is mostly incorrect; less than 5% of plant mass comes from soil minerals; the rest comes from CO2 and water  C. Van Helmont's experiment was flawed  D. Plants get food from sunlight, not soil
**Correct: B**

### Question 4
In a bright greenhouse with plenty of water, CO2 concentration is the limiting factor. What will most increase plant growth?
A. Adding more light  B. Adding more water  C. Supplementing CO2 to higher concentrations  D. Adding more soil
**Correct: C** — Addressing the limiting factor removes the bottleneck.

### Question 5
Plants perform photosynthesis during the day and cellular respiration 24 hours a day. At night without light, what happens?
A. Plants release oxygen at night just like during the day  B. Plants absorb oxygen and release CO2 because they are only doing cellular respiration  C. Plants stop all chemical activity at night  D. Photosynthesis continues using stored light
**Correct: B**

### Answer Key
Q1: C | Q2: B | Q3: B | Q4: C | Q5: B
