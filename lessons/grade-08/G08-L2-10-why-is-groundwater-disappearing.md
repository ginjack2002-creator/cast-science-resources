## Platform

### Title
**Why Is Groundwater Disappearing? — How Human Activity and Natural Cycles Determine the Future of Underground Water**

### Overview
Groundwater is disappearing because humans are pumping water out of aquifers far faster than nature can replace it. Agricultural irrigation is the biggest consumer, and rainfall recharge is a slow process that can take years to centuries. When aquifer levels drop, the rock and soil above compact and... Students build a cause-and-effect model exploring how agricultural irrigation and rainfall recharge interact with aquifer level, land subsidence, and long-term water security in this system.

### NGSS Standard
**MS-ESS3-1, MS-ESS2-4**: Construct a scientific explanation based on evidence for how the uneven distributions of Earth's mineral, energy, and groundwater resources are the result of past and current geoscience processes. Develop a model to describe the cycling of water through Earth's systems driven by energy from the sun and the force of gravity.

### Learning Objectives
- Explain how agricultural irrigation and rainfall recharge affect aquifer levels
- Model the relationship between water extraction and long-term aquifer sustainability
- Predict how land subsidence occurs when underground water is removed
- Evaluate evidence for why groundwater depletion threatens long-term water security

### Component List (Starting Model: 5 Components)

|-----------|------|-------------------|
| Agricultural Irrigation | External | Volume of groundwater pumped for farming — the primary demand on aquifers |
| Rainfall Recharge | External | Amount of precipitation that percolates down to replenish the aquifer |
| Aquifer Level | Internal | Current water table height — decreases when pumping exceeds natural recharge |
| Land Subsidence | Internal | Ground surface sinking as empty underground spaces compact under pressure |
| Long-term Water Security | Internal | Projected ability to meet future water needs based on current extraction rates |

## CAST-Aligned Pre/Post Assessment

### Administration Instructions

These 5 multiple-choice questions are administered identically as both a pre-assessment (before Activity 1) and a post-assessment (after Activity 4). Score each out of 5. Learning growth = post-score minus pre-score.

Questions follow the California Science Test (CAST) stimulus-response format. Each item is three-dimensional, assessing a Science and Engineering Practice (SEP), Disciplinary Core Idea (DCI), and Crosscutting Concept (CCC) simultaneously, aligned to MS-ESS3-1, MS-ESS2-4.

### Question 1

CAST Alignment: SEP 2.1.1 (Determine components of a system) + DCI + CCC4 (Describe a system in terms of its components)

A scientist is studying why is groundwater disappearing?. She identifies these components: Agricultural Irrigation, Rainfall Recharge, Aquifer Level, Land Subsidence, Long-term Water Security. She sorts them into external (things we set or cannot control) and internal (things that change based on other factors).

Which list correctly sorts the components?

A. External: Agricultural Irrigation, Aquifer Level — Internal: Rainfall Recharge, Land Subsidence, Long-term Water Security
B. External: Agricultural Irrigation, Rainfall Recharge — Internal: Aquifer Level, Land Subsidence, Long-term Water Security
C. External: Aquifer Level, Land Subsidence — Internal: Agricultural Irrigation, Rainfall Recharge, Long-term Water Security
D. External: Long-term Water Security, Agricultural Irrigation — Internal: Rainfall Recharge, Aquifer Level, Land Subsidence

Correct Answer: B

Feedback: Agricultural Irrigation and Rainfall Recharge are external because they represent conditions that drive the system. Aquifer Level, Land Subsidence, and Long-term Water Security are internal because they change based on other factors in the system.

### Question 2

CAST Alignment: SEP 2.1.2 (Determine relationships among components) + DCI + CCC4 (Describe components and interactions)

A farming region pumps 500 million gallons per year from its aquifer. Rainfall recharges the aquifer at 200 million gallons per year. At this rate, which of the following will occur, and why?

A. Agricultural Irrigation has no effect on Aquifer Level
B. As Agricultural Irrigation increases, Aquifer Level decreases
C. As Agricultural Irrigation decreases, Aquifer Level decreases
D. Aquifer Level controls Agricultural Irrigation, not the other way around

Correct Answer: B

Feedback: More pumping for irrigation removes water from the aquifer, lowering the water table.
