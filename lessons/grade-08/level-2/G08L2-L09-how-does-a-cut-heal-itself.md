### Title
**How Does a Cut Heal Itself? — How Your Body Orchestrates Cell Division to Repair Damaged Tissue**

### Overview
Your body heals a cut through a carefully coordinated process. When tissue is damaged, chemical signals trigger nearby cells to increase their division rate (mitosis). Blood supply delivers the oxygen and nutrients these rapidly dividing cells need. New cells gradually fill the wound, forming new ti... Students build a cause-and-effect model exploring how wound severity and blood supply interact with cell division rate, new tissue formation, and healing progress in this system.

### NGSS Standard
**MS-LS1-4, MS-LS1-2**: Use argument based on empirical evidence and scientific reasoning to support an explanation for how characteristic animal behaviors and specialized plant structures affect the probability of successful reproduction of animals and plants respectively. Develop and use a model to describe the function of a cell as a whole and ways the parts of cells contribute to the function.

### Learning Objectives
- Explain how wound severity and blood supply affect the healing process
- Model the relationship between cell division rate and new tissue formation
- Predict how different wound conditions affect healing progress over time
- Evaluate evidence for why some wounds heal faster than others

### Component List (Starting Model: 5 Components)

| Component | Type | What It Represents |
|-----------|------|-------------------|
| Wound Severity | External | The depth and area of tissue damage — sets the scale of repair needed |
| Blood Supply | External | Blood flow to the wound area delivering oxygen, nutrients, and immune cells |
| Cell Division Rate | Internal | How rapidly cells undergo mitosis to produce replacement cells |
| New Tissue Formation | Internal | Growth of new skin, blood vessels, and connective tissue at the wound site |
| Healing Progress | Internal | Overall wound closure and tissue restoration over time |

This is called a CONDITIONAL RELATIONSHIP — the connection
between two components depends on a third component's state.

Task A: SET THE CONDITION
   • Click on the connection arrow from Wound Severity to Cell Division Rate
   • In the toolbar, click "Conditions"
   • In the conditions panel, set: IF Blood Supply is ON
   • Click "Save Conditions"

## CAST-Aligned Pre/Post Assessment

### Administration Instructions

These 5 multiple-choice questions are administered identically as both a pre-assessment (before Activity 1) and a post-assessment (after Activity 4). Score each out of 5. Learning growth = post-score minus pre-score.

Questions follow the California Science Test (CAST) stimulus-response format. Each item is three-dimensional, assessing a Science and Engineering Practice (SEP), Disciplinary Core Idea (DCI), and Crosscutting Concept (CCC) simultaneously, aligned to MS-LS1-4, MS-LS1-2.

---

### Question 1

CAST Alignment: SEP 2.1.1 (Determine components of a system) + DCI + CCC4 (Describe a system in terms of its components)

A scientist is studying how does a cut heal itself?. She identifies these components: Wound Severity, Blood Supply, Cell Division Rate, New Tissue Formation, Healing Progress. She sorts them into external (things we set or cannot control) and internal (things that change based on other factors).

Which list correctly sorts the components?

A. External: Cell Division Rate, New Tissue Formation — Internal: Wound Severity, Blood Supply, Healing Progress
B. External: Healing Progress, Wound Severity — Internal: Blood Supply, Cell Division Rate, New Tissue Formation
C. External: Wound Severity, Cell Division Rate — Internal: Blood Supply, New Tissue Formation, Healing Progress
D. External: Wound Severity, Blood Supply — Internal: Cell Division Rate, New Tissue Formation, Healing Progress

Correct Answer: D

Feedback: Wound Severity and Blood Supply are external because they represent conditions that drive the system. Cell Division Rate, New Tissue Formation, and Healing Progress are internal because they change based on other factors in the system.
---

### Question 2

CAST Alignment: SEP 2.1.2 (Determine relationships among components) + DCI + CCC4 (Describe components and interactions)

Patient A has a moderate cut on their well-supplied forearm. Patient B has the same cut on their lower leg, where circulation is reduced due to diabetes. After 10 days, which patient will show more healing progress, and what is the primary reason?

A. As Wound Severity increases, Cell Division Rate increases
B. Wound Severity has no effect on Cell Division Rate
C. As Wound Severity decreases, Cell Division Rate increases
D. Cell Division Rate controls Wound Severity, not the other way around

Correct Answer: A

Feedback: More severe wounds trigger stronger chemical signals (growth factors) that increase the rate of cell division at the wound edge.
