### Title
**Why Does Your Bike Rust? — How Water and Oxygen Change Metal Over Time**

### Overview
Your bike rusts because of a CHEMICAL REACTION between the iron in the metal, water, and oxygen from the air! When water sits on the metal surface, it helps oxygen molecules react with iron atoms. This reaction creates a completely new substance called iron oxide — what we call RUST. Rust is not jus... Students build a cause-and-effect model exploring how water exposure and oxygen level interact with oxidation rate, rust formation, and metal strength in this system.

### Learning Objectives
- Explain how water and oxygen interact with metal surfaces to create a new substance called rust
- Model how water exposure, oxygen level, oxidation rate, rust formation, and metal strength interact in a system
- Describe how rust is a chemical change that creates a new, weaker substance from the original strong metal
- Use evidence from a model to explain why protecting metal from water and oxygen prevents rust

### Component List (Starting Model: 5 Components)

| Component | Type | What It Represents |
|-----------|------|-------------------|
| Water Exposure | External | How much water comes into contact with the metal surface, from rain, humidity, splashing, or being submerged. More water means more opportunity for rust to form |
| Oxygen Level | External | How much oxygen is available to react with the metal. Air is about 21% oxygen, and water contains dissolved oxygen too |
| Oxidation Rate | Internal | How quickly the chemical reaction between iron, water, and oxygen occurs. Higher water and oxygen levels speed up this reaction |
| Rust Formation | Internal | How much iron oxide (rust) has formed on the metal surface. Rust is a new substance that is weaker and flakier than the original metal |
| Metal Strength | Internal | How strong and structurally sound the metal remains. As rust replaces solid metal, the object becomes weaker and can eventually break |

## CAST-Aligned Pre/Post Assessment

### Administration Instructions

These 5 multiple-choice questions are administered identically as both a pre-assessment (before Activity 1) and a post-assessment (after Activity 4). Score each out of 5. Learning growth = post-score minus pre-score.

Questions follow the California Science Test (CAST) stimulus-response format. Each item is three-dimensional, assessing a Science and Engineering Practice (SEP), Disciplinary Core Idea (DCI), and Crosscutting Concept (CCC) simultaneously, aligned to 4-PS1-2, 4-ETS1-1.

---

### Question 1

CAST Alignment: SEP 2.1.1 (Determine components of a system) + DCI + CCC4 (Describe a system in terms of its components)

A scientist is studying why does your bike rust?. She identifies these components: Water Exposure, Oxygen Level, Oxidation Rate, Rust Formation, Metal Strength. She sorts them into external (things we set or cannot control) and internal (things that change based on other factors).

Which list correctly sorts the components?

A. External: Water Exposure, Oxidation Rate — Internal: Oxygen Level, Rust Formation, Metal Strength
B. External: Water Exposure, Oxygen Level — Internal: Oxidation Rate, Rust Formation, Metal Strength
C. External: Oxidation Rate, Rust Formation — Internal: Water Exposure, Oxygen Level, Metal Strength
D. External: Metal Strength, Water Exposure — Internal: Oxygen Level, Oxidation Rate, Rust Formation

Correct Answer: B

Feedback: Water Exposure and Oxygen Level are external because they represent conditions that drive the system. Oxidation Rate, Rust Formation, and Metal Strength are internal because they change based on other factors in the system.
