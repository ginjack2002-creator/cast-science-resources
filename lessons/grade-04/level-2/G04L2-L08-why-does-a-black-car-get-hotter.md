### Title
**Why Does a Black Car Get Hotter? — How Surface Color Affects Heat Absorption from Sunlight**

### Overview
A black car gets hotter because dark colors ABSORB most of the light energy from the sun instead of reflecting it! When sunlight hits a dark surface, the light energy is captured and converted into THERMAL ENERGY (heat). The surface temperature rises because all that absorbed energy has nowhere to g... Students build a cause-and-effect model exploring how sunlight intensity and surface color interact with heat absorption, surface temperature, and comfort level in this system.

### NGSS Standard
**4-PS3-2**: Make observations to provide evidence that energy can be transferred from place to place by sound, light, thermal energy, and electric currents.

### Learning Objectives
- Explain how sunlight transfers energy to surfaces and how that energy becomes heat
- Model how sunlight intensity, surface color, heat absorption, surface temperature, and comfort level interact in a system
- Describe how darker surfaces absorb more light energy and convert it to heat compared to lighter surfaces
- Use evidence from a model to explain why color matters for staying cool in the sun

### Component List (Starting Model: 5 Components)

| Component | Type | What It Represents |
|-----------|------|-------------------|
| Sunlight Intensity | External | How strong the sunlight is, which depends on time of day, cloud cover, and season. Stronger sunlight delivers more energy to surfaces |
| Surface Color | External | How dark or light the surface is. Dark colors like black absorb most light energy, while light colors like white reflect most of it |
| Heat Absorption | Internal | The amount of light energy that a surface captures and converts into thermal energy instead of reflecting it back |
| Surface Temperature | Internal | How hot the surface becomes after absorbing light energy. More absorption means a higher temperature |
| Comfort Level | Internal | How comfortable a person feels when touching or sitting on the surface. Hotter surfaces are less comfortable |

## CAST-Aligned Pre/Post Assessment

### Administration Instructions

These 5 multiple-choice questions are administered identically as both a pre-assessment (before Activity 1) and a post-assessment (after Activity 4). Score each out of 5. Learning growth = post-score minus pre-score.

Questions follow the California Science Test (CAST) stimulus-response format. Each item is three-dimensional, assessing a Science and Engineering Practice (SEP), Disciplinary Core Idea (DCI), and Crosscutting Concept (CCC) simultaneously, aligned to 4-PS3-2.

---

### Question 1

CAST Alignment: SEP 2.1.1 (Determine components of a system) + DCI + CCC4 (Describe a system in terms of its components)

A scientist is studying why does a black car get hotter?. She identifies these components: Sunlight Intensity, Surface Color, Heat Absorption, Surface Temperature, Comfort Level. She sorts them into external (things we set or cannot control) and internal (things that change based on other factors).

Which list correctly sorts the components?

A. External: Sunlight Intensity, Surface Color — Internal: Heat Absorption, Surface Temperature, Comfort Level
B. External: Sunlight Intensity, Heat Absorption — Internal: Surface Color, Surface Temperature, Comfort Level
C. External: Comfort Level, Sunlight Intensity — Internal: Surface Color, Heat Absorption, Surface Temperature
D. External: Heat Absorption, Surface Temperature — Internal: Sunlight Intensity, Surface Color, Comfort Level

Correct Answer: A

Feedback: Sunlight Intensity and Surface Color are external because they represent conditions that drive the system. Heat Absorption, Surface Temperature, and Comfort Level are internal because they change based on other factors in the system.
