## Platform

### Title
**Why Do Some Things Dissolve and Others Don't? — How Water Temperature and Motion Affect Dissolving Speed**

### Overview
Some things dissolve faster because of particle contact rate — how often water particles bump into the solid. Hot water has faster-moving particles that collide more often, and stirring brings fresh water to the solid surface constantly. Both increase the rate at which the solid breaks apart and mix... Students build a cause-and-effect model exploring how water temperature and stirring speed interact with particle contact rate, dissolving speed, and solution concentration in this system.

### Grade
5th

### NGSS Standard
**5-PS1-1, 5-PS1-4**: Develop a model to describe that matter is made of particles too small to be seen. Conduct an investigation to determine whether the mixing of two or more substances results in new substances.

### Learning Objectives
- Model how water temperature and stirring speed affect dissolving rate
- Explain how particle contact rate determines how quickly a substance dissolves
- Predict how changing temperature or stirring speed affects solution concentration
- Connect particle-level interactions to observable dissolving behavior

### Component List (Starting Model: 5 Components)

| Component | Type | What It Represents |
|-----------|------|-------------------|
| Water Temperature | External | The temperature of the water used as the solvent |
| Stirring Speed | External | How fast the water is being stirred or agitated |
| Particle Contact Rate | Internal | How often water particles bump into and interact with the solid particles |
| Dissolving Speed | Internal | How quickly the solid breaks apart and mixes into the water |
| Solution Concentration | Internal | How much of the solid has dissolved into the water |

COMMON MISCONCEPTIONS:
• "Dissolved substances disappear" → When a solid dissolves, it does not vanish. Its particles separate and mix evenly with the water par
• "Stirring creates heat that dissolves things" → Stirring does not significantly heat the water. It works by moving fresh, unsaturated water to the s

ASSESSMENT:

• Does student's model show correct positive/negative relationships?
• Did student add at least one researched component?

DIFFERENTIATION:

## CAST-Aligned Pre/Post Assessment

### Administration Instructions

These 5 multiple-choice questions are administered identically as both a pre-assessment (before Activity 1) and a post-assessment (after Activity 4). Score each out of 5. Learning growth = post-score minus pre-score.

Questions follow the California Science Test (CAST) stimulus-response format. Each item is three-dimensional, assessing a Science and Engineering Practice (SEP), Disciplinary Core Idea (DCI), and Crosscutting Concept (CCC) simultaneously, aligned to 5-PS1-1, 5-PS1-4.

---

### Question 1

CAST Alignment: SEP 2.1.1 (Determine components of a system) + DCI + CCC4 (Describe a system in terms of its components)

A scientist is studying why do some things dissolve and others don't?. She identifies these components: Water Temperature, Stirring Speed, Particle Contact Rate, Dissolving Speed, Solution Concentration. She sorts them into external (things we set or cannot control) and internal (things that change based on other factors).

Which list correctly sorts the components?

A. External: Solution Concentration, Water Temperature — Internal: Stirring Speed, Particle Contact Rate, Dissolving Speed
B. External: Water Temperature, Particle Contact Rate — Internal: Stirring Speed, Dissolving Speed, Solution Concentration
C. External: Particle Contact Rate, Dissolving Speed — Internal: Water Temperature, Stirring Speed, Solution Concentration
D. External: Water Temperature, Stirring Speed — Internal: Particle Contact Rate, Dissolving Speed, Solution Concentration

Correct Answer: D

Feedback: Water Temperature and Stirring Speed are external because they represent conditions that drive the system. Particle Contact Rate, Dissolving Speed, and Solution Concentration are internal because they change based on other factors in the system.
---

### Question 2

CAST Alignment: SEP 2.1.2 (Determine relationships among components) + DCI + CCC4 (Describe components and interactions)

A student puts identical sugar cubes in two cups of water. Cup A is hot and stirred. Cup B is cold and not stirred. Which sugar cube dissolves first and what is the MAIN reason?

A. As Water Temperature increases, Particle Contact Rate increases
B. Water Temperature has no effect on Particle Contact Rate
C. As Water Temperature decreases, Particle Contact Rate increases
D. Particle Contact Rate controls Water Temperature, not the other way around

Correct Answer: A

Feedback: Warmer water particles move faster, colliding with the solid surface more frequently.
