## Platform

### Title
**Can a River Change Direction? — How Flowing Water Reshapes the Land Over Time**

### Overview
Yes, a river can change direction! Fast-moving water erodes soft soil on the outside of bends, while slower water on the inside deposits sediment. Over time, this makes bends grow larger and the river shifts its path. In very soft soil with fast water, rivers can change course dramatically.... Students build a cause-and-effect model exploring how water flow speed and soil softness interact with erosion rate, sediment transport, and channel shape change in this system.

### NGSS Standard
**5-ESS2-1, 5-ESS2-2**: Develop a model using an example to describe ways the geosphere, hydrosphere, atmosphere, and biosphere interact. Describe and graph the amounts and percentages of water and fresh water in various reservoirs to provide evidence about the distribution of water on Earth.

### Learning Objectives
- Model how water flow speed and soil softness affect erosion and sediment transport
- Explain how erosion on one bank and deposition on another changes a river's path
- Predict how changes in water flow speed affect channel shape over time
- Connect river processes to real-world landform changes

### Component List (Starting Model: 5 Components)

| Component | Type | What It Represents |
|-----------|------|-------------------|
| Water Flow Speed | External | How fast the water moves through the river channel |
| Soil Softness | External | How easily the riverbank material can be worn away by water |
| Erosion Rate | Internal | How quickly the river wears away soil and rock from its banks |
| Sediment Transport | Internal | How much loose material the river carries as it flows |
| Channel Shape Change | Internal | How much the river's path shifts over time due to erosion and deposition |

## CAST-Aligned Pre/Post Assessment

### Administration Instructions

These 5 multiple-choice questions are administered identically as both a pre-assessment (before Activity 1) and a post-assessment (after Activity 4). Score each out of 5. Learning growth = post-score minus pre-score.

Questions follow the California Science Test (CAST) stimulus-response format. Each item is three-dimensional, assessing a Science and Engineering Practice (SEP), Disciplinary Core Idea (DCI), and Crosscutting Concept (CCC) simultaneously, aligned to 5-ESS2-1, 5-ESS2-2.

---

### Question 1

CAST Alignment: SEP 2.1.1 (Determine components of a system) + DCI + CCC4 (Describe a system in terms of its components)

A scientist is studying can a river change direction?. She identifies these components: Water Flow Speed, Soil Softness, Erosion Rate, Sediment Transport, Channel Shape Change. She sorts them into external (things we set or cannot control) and internal (things that change based on other factors).

Which list correctly sorts the components?

A. External: Water Flow Speed, Erosion Rate — Internal: Soil Softness, Sediment Transport, Channel Shape Change
B. External: Water Flow Speed, Soil Softness — Internal: Erosion Rate, Sediment Transport, Channel Shape Change
C. External: Erosion Rate, Sediment Transport — Internal: Water Flow Speed, Soil Softness, Channel Shape Change
D. External: Channel Shape Change, Water Flow Speed — Internal: Soil Softness, Erosion Rate, Sediment Transport

Correct Answer: B

Feedback: Water Flow Speed and Soil Softness are external because they represent conditions that drive the system. Erosion Rate, Sediment Transport, and Channel Shape Change are internal because they change based on other factors in the system.

### Question 2

CAST Alignment: SEP 2.1.2 (Determine relationships among components) + DCI + CCC4 (Describe components and interactions)

A river flows through an area where one bank is soft clay and the other is solid rock. Over time, which direction will the river shift and why?

A. Water Flow Speed has no effect on Erosion Rate
B. As Water Flow Speed increases, Erosion Rate increases
C. As Water Flow Speed decreases, Erosion Rate increases
D. Erosion Rate controls Water Flow Speed, not the other way around

Correct Answer: B

Feedback: Faster water has more energy to dislodge and carry away soil and rock particles.
