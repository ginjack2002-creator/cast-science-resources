What is a Component?
A component is a part of a system that plays a specific role. In our wildfire model, each component affects how fire behaves. Components can be EXTERNAL (come from outside the system) or INTERNAL (happen within the system).

Research & Extend
ModelIt!
Real systems are more complex than our model! Here are additional components scientists study. Choose one and explain how it could connect to our model.

Possible New Components

  • Temperature — Higher temperatures dry out vegetation faster and can create updrafts that spread fire
  • Humidity — Low humidity means drier air, which helps fire spread more easily
  • Terrain / Slopes — Fire travels uphill faster because heat rises and preheats vegetation above
  • Santa Ana Winds — Hot, dry winds specific to California that dramatically increase fire danger
  • Human Activity — Power lines, campfires, and development near wildlands can start fires

My Extension

A Which new component would you add to the model?
B How would it connect to the existing components? (Draw or describe)
C What evidence or reasoning supports your connection?

YOUR ENGINEERING MISSION

The town of Willow Creek is surrounded by dry forest. A wildfire has been spotted 5 miles away and winds are pushing it toward town. Your team must design a firebreak system to protect the community!

THE SCENARIO
  • Willow Creek has 2,000 residents, a school, and a hospital
  • Dense, dry forest surrounds the town on 3 sides

  • Prevailing winds blow from the northwest
