# ModelIt! Research Foundation

## The Science Behind ModelIt!

ModelIt! is built on Cell Collective, a research-grade computational modeling platform created by Dr. Tomas Helikar at the University of Nebraska-Lincoln. The platform and its pedagogical approach are backed by 130+ peer-reviewed publications. This document summarizes the key research that validates ModelIt!'s approach to K-12 science education.

---

## Key Research Findings

### 1. Simulation Changes How Students' Brains Process Science
**Clark, Helikar & Dauer (2020)** — *CBE—Life Sciences Education*

Using fMRI neuroimaging, researchers found that students who simulated computational biological models showed significantly higher brain activation in visuospatial processing and interoceptive awareness regions compared to students who simply read expert analysis. Even when test scores appeared similar, the simulation group was building fundamentally different — and deeper — neural representations of biological systems.

**What this means for ModelIt!:** When students use ModelIt! to simulate and manipulate models, they are literally building different brain pathways than traditional instruction provides. This is neuroscience-level validation that hands-on computational modeling produces deeper learning.

---

### 2. Measurable Learning Gains with No Gender Bias
**Booth et al. (2021)** — *CBE—Life Sciences Education*

Students using Cell Collective's interactive predict-observe-explain modules showed an **8% learning gain** compared to **0% gain** for the control group. Importantly, the modules did not create or reinforce gender bias — a critical finding for equitable STEM education.

**What this means for ModelIt!:** The same platform powering ModelIt! has been proven to produce measurable improvements in student understanding. The predict-observe-explain framework used in this study is embedded in every ModelIt! lesson pack.

---

### 3. The Gap ModelIt! Fills: Research-Grade Yet Accessible
**Helikar (2021)** — *Trends in Molecular Medicine*

Written during the COVID-19 pandemic, Dr. Helikar argued that students need access to research-grade modeling tools, not simplified educational toys. Existing tools are either too simple (pre-built simulations like PhET with no model-building) or too complex (research software requiring programming). NGSS mandates computational modeling and systems thinking, but most schools lack appropriate tools.

**What this means for ModelIt!:** ModelIt! is the direct answer to this identified gap. It provides research-grade scientific authenticity with an interface that K-12 students can use without programming or advanced mathematics.

---

### 4. Traditional Instruction Fails at Systems Thinking
**Helikar et al. (2016)** — Research-driven facilitation of systems thinking

Students receiving traditional lecture instruction could recall more individual components of cellular respiration, but were **no better** at integrating those components into a systems-level understanding. Computational modeling interventions specifically improved mechanistic reasoning.

**What this means for ModelIt!:** Lecture-based science produces students who can list parts but cannot reason about how those parts interact — the exact gap that NGSS standards aim to close. ModelIt!'s simulation-based approach directly addresses this documented failure of traditional methods.

---

### 5. Instructors Adopt Because It Helps Students Learn
**Song, Helikar, Smith & Helikar (2023)** — *CBE—Life Sciences Education*

A study of 43 instructors across 37 institutions found that features improving student learning had the **strongest influence** on adoption decisions (effect size = 0.309). Teaching-convenience features showed no significant impact on adoption attitudes.

**What this means for ModelIt!:** Educators choose tools that demonstrably help students learn. ModelIt!'s interactive simulations and real-time feedback — the features that most directly impact student understanding — are what drive adoption.

---

### 6. Proven Curriculum Integration Models
**Helikar et al. (2015)** — *PLoS Computational Biology*

Cell Collective was successfully implemented both as a standalone "In Silico Biology" course and as modules integrated into existing biology courses. Student work led to conference presentations and peer-reviewed publications, demonstrating authentic scientific experiences.

**What this means for ModelIt!:** ModelIt!'s lesson packs (Student Presentation, Activity Pack, Teacher's Guide) are designed for seamless integration into existing NGSS-aligned science courses — the proven deployment model from this research.

---

### 7. Built on International Standards
**Keating et al. (2020)** — *Molecular Systems Biology*; **Chaouiya et al. (2013)** — *BMC Systems Biology*

Cell Collective uses SBML (Systems Biology Markup Language), the internationally recognized standard for biological model exchange. Dr. Helikar co-authored both the SBML Level 3 specification and the SBML qual extension for Boolean/logical models.

**What this means for ModelIt!:** Students working on ModelIt! interact with research-grade, standards-compliant models — the same format used by professional scientists worldwide. This is authentic science, not a simplified toy.

---

### 8. CURE-Compliant Infrastructure
**Sauro, Helikar et al. (2025)** — ArXiv (NIH-funded)

The CURE guidelines (Credible, Understandable, Reproducible, Extensible) represent emerging international standards for computational biological models. Cell Collective already embodies these principles through its SBML compatibility, visual interfaces, and model sharing capabilities.

**What this means for ModelIt!:** ModelIt! is built on infrastructure that meets the latest international standards for computational model quality — a powerful differentiator showing students work with scientifically authentic tools.

---

### 9. Skills That Lead to Real-World Careers
**Aghamiri, Amin & Helikar (2021)** — *Journal of Pharmacokinetics and Pharmacodynamics*; **Laubenbacher, Helikar et al. (2022)** — *NPJ Digital Medicine*

Computational systems modeling is driving breakthroughs in drug discovery, precision medicine, and digital twins of the human immune system. The same modeling skills students develop on ModelIt! are used in cutting-edge biotech and pharmaceutical research.

**What this means for ModelIt!:** ModelIt! gives K-12 students early exposure to the computational modeling methods used in today's most advanced healthcare research, building skills for tomorrow's STEM careers.
