# ModelIt Lesson Template

## Instructions for Lesson Creators

Use this template to create NGSS-aligned, phenomenon-based ModelIt lessons. Follow the LEVER framework flow:
- **Step 1**: LOCATE — Build components
- **Step 2**: ESTABLISH — Connect relationships
- **Step 3**: VISUALIZE/EVALUATE — Simulate and observe
- **Step 4**: REVISE/EXTEND — Play, research, expand

### Key Requirements
- [ ] High-interest phenomenon (relatable, surprising, engaging)
- [ ] NGSS/CAST aligned
- [ ] Simple starting model (3-4 components)
- [ ] Room for student research and expansion
- [ ] Works as unit/lesson HOOK
- [ ] Boolean-compatible (cause-effect, pathways, feedback)
- [ ] Brainchild style language (fun, conversational, not condescending)

## Step 3: VISUALIZE & EVALUATE — Run Your Model

### Text Editor

```
[HOOK — Time to test it! 1-2 sentences]

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Task A: RUN THE SIMULATION
• Click the "Play" button in the TOP LEFT corner
• Watch the graph panel — you'll see percentage lines for each component

Task B: OBSERVE THE BASELINE
• Let it run for about 30 time steps
• Notice how the lines relate to each other
• When [Component A] is HIGH (100%), what happens to [Component B]?
• When [Component B] is [HIGH/LOW], what happens to [Component C]?

Task C: CHANGE A VARIABLE
• Look at the RIGHT PANEL where your components are listed
• Find the LOCK icon (🔒) next to "[Component A]"
• Click the lock to TURN [ON/OFF] [Component A] (set to [0/100]%)
• This simulates [what real-world scenario?]

Task D: WATCH THE CASCADE
• Resume the simulation
• What happens to [Component B] when [condition]?
• What happens to [Component C]?

Task E: TRY ANOTHER SCENARIO
• Lock "[Component D]" to [ON/OFF] ([0/100]%)
• This simulates [what?]
• What changes now?

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

REFLECTION QUESTIONS:
• What combination creates the [MOST/WORST] [outcome]?
• What would need to change to [alternative outcome]?
• Does your model match what happens in the real world?
```

### Video Script

```
"[Opening — excitement about testing, 1-2 sentences]

[Describe baseline behavior — what should students see when things are "normal"?]

components are listed. See the little lock icon next to [Component]?
Click it. Set [Component] to [value] percent.

This is [what real-world scenario this simulates].

[Explain what's happening and why]

[Describe the cascade effect — dominoes falling]

[Add second scenario if applicable]

Look at [both graphs / the pattern]. [Key insight about the system]

You just simulated [real-world phenomenon]!

Now it's your turn to ModelIt!"
```

## Step 4: REVISE & EXTEND — Play, Research, Expand

### Text Editor

```
YOUR MODEL WORKS — BUT IT'S NOT COMPLETE

[Acknowledge what they built, but hint at complexity. 2-3 sentences]

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

PLAY TIME CHALLENGES:

1. TELL THE STORY
   • Run your simulation
   • [Role-play scenario]
   • Explain what's happening and WHY to your partner

2. BREAK THE SYSTEM
   • What happens if you [change something]?
   • What happens if [another scenario]?
   • Can you find a combination where [alternative outcome]?

3. WHAT'S MISSING?
   Your model is simple. Real [phenomena] involve more. Think about:

   • [Missing factor 1] — how might this connect?
   • [Missing factor 2] — where does this fit?
   • [Missing factor 3] — how would you model something that [does X]?
   • [Missing factor 4] — [question]
   • [Missing factor 5] — [question]

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

RESEARCH DIRECTIONS:

Don't just guess — find out! Here's what to look for:

📚 IN YOUR TEXTBOOK:
   • [Topic they should look up]
   • [Related concept from curriculum]
   • [Connection to other units]

🔍 QUESTIONS TO INVESTIGATE:
   • [Research question 1]
   • [Research question 2]
   • [Research question 3]
   • [Research question 4]

🌐 RELIABLE SOURCES:
   • [Website 1]: [URL]
   • [Website 2]: [URL]
   • [Website 3]: [URL]

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

ADD TO YOUR MODEL:
   • Pick ONE new component from your research
   • Decide: Is it INTERNAL or EXTERNAL?
   • Add it to your model (Plus button)
   • Connect it with relationships (+/−)
   • Run the simulation — does it work like you expected?

What story does your NEW model tell?
```

### Video Script

```
"[Acknowledge their model works. 1-2 sentences]

[Hint at complexity — real world is more complicated. 1-2 sentences]

First — tell the story. [Role-play prompt]. If you can explain it,
you understand it.

Second — break the system. [Specific things to try]. Play with the
locks and see what you discover.

Third — and this is the big one — ask what's MISSING.

Your model has [X] components. Real [phenomenon] involves way more.
Think about...

[List 2-3 missing factors with guiding questions]

Here's your mission: Research ONE new factor. Find out how it
actually works in the real world. Then add it to your model.

Is it internal or external? Click the plus button to add it.
Draw the connections. Set positive or negative. Run the simulation.

Does your new model match reality better than before?

This is how real scientists work. Start simple. Test it. Add
complexity. Test again. Your model is never 'done' — it's always
getting better.

[Closing question]

Now it's your turn to ModelIt!"
```
